How Schedule 1-A Works — The Key Thing Most People Miss
Schedule 1-A deductions are "below-the-line" — they reduce your taxable income AFTER your AGI is calculated. That means they work on top of your standard or itemized deduction. You don't have to choose — you get both. The total from Part VI flows to Form 1040, Line 12b. You MUST attach Schedule 1-A to your return. Full Explanation →

MAGI is Different From AGI — Important!
For Schedule 1-A, MAGI includes certain income typically excluded (foreign income, etc.). All 4 deductions use your MAGI to calculate phase-outs. Enter your income below and TaxSavvy calculates MAGI automatically for each part.
🍽️ Part II — No Tax on Tips Schedule 1-A, Lines 3–10
IRS Tips Guide ↗ Tip Occupations ↗
Who qualifies: Employees and self-employed in occupations that customarily and regularly received tips on or before Dec. 31, 2024 (e.g., servers, bartenders, salon workers, hotel staff, valets, personal trainers, casino dealers, gig drivers). Check the full list at IRS.gov/TippedOccupations.
NOT eligible: Attorneys, accountants, doctors, consultants, financial advisors, or anyone in a Specified Service Trade or Business (SSTB).
Max deduction: $25,000 (single) · Phase-out starts at $150,000 MAGI ($300,000 MFJ) · Must have valid SSN · If married, must file jointly
Source: IRC §224 · OBBBA 2025
📋
2025 Transition Note — Notice 2025-62
For 2025, tips may not be separately reported on your W-2. If Box 7 of your W-2 shows social security tips, you can use that amount. If you keep a daily tip log (required by IRS), use your log totals. Notice 2025-62 →

⏰ Part III — No Tax on Overtime Schedule 1-A, Lines 11–21
IRS Q&A ↗ Notice 2025-69 ↗
Who qualifies: Employees covered by and NOT exempt from the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) who received overtime pay at time-and-a-half or more for hours over 40/week. Also applies to some self-employed individuals with FLSA-covered work.
NOT eligible: Exempt employees (managers, executives, professionals earning over $58,656/year exempt threshold), independent contractors not under FLSA.
What counts: ONLY the "half" of time-and-a-half — the premium above your regular rate. NOT the base rate portion of overtime hours.
2025 special rule: Your employer may not have reported this on W-2 Box 14. You may need to calculate it yourself from payroll stubs.
Max deduction: $12,500 (single) / $25,000 (MFJ) · Phase-out starts $150,000 MAGI ($300,000 MFJ) · Must have valid SSN · If married, must file jointly
Source: IRC §225 · OBBBA 2025 · FLSA Section 7 · Notice 2025-69
🔍
How to Find Your Overtime Premium (What Goes on Schedule 1-A)
If W-2 Box 14 shows overtime separately: Use that number directly.
If paid "1.5x" and Box 14 is blank: Total overtime hours pay ÷ 3 = your premium (the "half" portion).
Example: You earned $9,000 total overtime at 1.5x. Your premium = $9,000 ÷ 3 = $3,000 deductible.
If paid "2x" overtime: Total overtime ÷ 4 = your premium.
Keep payroll stubs as documentation. IRS Notice 2025-69 has all the calculation methods →
📉 Phase-Out Calculation (MAGI over threshold)
Your Line 21 — Overtime Deduction: $0 (not eligible)
🚗 Part IV — Car Loan Interest Schedule 1-A, Lines 22–30
IRS Details ↗ IRS Withholding ↗
Who qualifies: Purchased a new, US-assembled passenger vehicle using a loan originated after December 31, 2024 (the loan must be new in 2025). Vehicle must be for personal use (not business use — that goes on Schedule C). You need the VIN to verify US assembly.
NOT eligible: Used cars · Leases · Business-use vehicles · Trucks/vans over 6,000 lbs gross weight if not passenger vehicles · Loans originated before 2025 · Foreign-assembled vehicles.
Max deduction: $10,000 per year · Phase-out starts at $100,000 MAGI ($200,000 MFJ) · Must have valid SSN
Source: IRC §226 · OBBBA 2025
💡
How to Find Your 2025 Interest Paid
Check your year-end loan statement from the lender showing total interest for 2025. Some lenders send a Form 1098 — but unlike mortgage interest, a 1098 is NOT required for car loans. You may need to request this from your lender directly or calculate from monthly statements.
📉 Phase-Out Calculation (MAGI over threshold)
Your Line 30 — Car Interest Deduction: $0 (not eligible)
👴 Part V — Enhanced Senior Deduction Schedule 1-A, Lines 31–37
IRS Details ↗
Who qualifies: Any taxpayer who was age 65 or older on or before December 31, 2025 (born January 2, 1960 or earlier for single; born January 2, 1961 or earlier on TurboTax's guidance). This is $6,000 per eligible person — if both spouses are 65+, that's $12,000 total. This is IN ADDITION to the existing standard deduction age-65 boost.
Available to ALL filers: Both standard deduction AND itemized deduction filers get this.
Phase-out: Starts at $75,000 MAGI (single) / $150,000 MAGI (MFJ). Reduces by $100 for each $1,000 over the threshold.
Source: IRC §227 · OBBBA 2025 · IRS Announcement
💡
This Is On Top of the Existing Senior Standard Deduction
The existing law already gives taxpayers 65+ an extra $1,950 (single) / $1,550 per person (MFJ) added to the standard deduction. The new OBBBA $6,000 deduction is completely separate and in addition to that. You get both. Most tax software may not automatically claim this new one — that's why TaxSavvy walks you through it.
📉 Phase-Out Calculation (MAGI over $75,000 single / $150,000 MFJ)
Your Line 37 — Senior Deduction: $0 (not eligible)

The home office deduction is frequently audited. Protect yourself:

✅ Floor plan sketch — showing office dimensions vs total home

💎 Billionaire Tax Playbook
10 legal strategies the ultra-wealthy use — now available to everyone · 100% IRS-compliant
✅ All 100% Legal
💎
Why Buffett Pays a Lower Rate Than His Secretary
The tax code rewards wealth over wages. Capital gains, depreciation, debt, and trusts are taxed at fractions of ordinary income rates. These aren't loopholes — they're features built by design. Every one of these strategies is 100% legal and IRS-compliant. TaxSavvy puts them all in your hands with direct links to the tax code.
🔄
Buy, Borrow, Die
💰 Eliminate capital gains tax completely
Buy appreciating assets, borrow against them (loans aren't taxable income), never sell. When heirs inherit, stepped-up basis wipes out all accumulated gains — forever. Used by Bezos, Musk, and Buffett.
IRC §1014 (Stepped-Up Basis) ↗
🏚️
1031 Like-Kind Exchange
💰 Defer all capital gains taxes — indefinitely
Sell real estate and roll 100% of gains into the next property. Zero tax due, forever. Pair with annual depreciation deductions even as properties appreciate. At death, heirs get stepped-up basis and gains are never taxed.
IRC §1031 / IRS Guide ↗
🏙️
Opportunity Zones (OBBBA Extended)
💰 100% of investment gains — tax-free forever
Invest capital gains in IRS-designated Opportunity Zones. Hold 10+ years and every dollar of appreciation is completely tax-free. The OBBBA extended this program through 2030+.
IRC §1400Z-2 / IRS OZ Page ↗
🚀
QSBS — Startup Stock Exclusion
💰 Up to $15M in gains completely tax-free
Invest in qualifying C-Corp startups and hold 5+ years. The OBBBA expanded the exclusion by 50% — now up to $15M or 10x basis excluded from ALL capital gains taxes.
IRC §1202 / IRS Pub 550 ↗
🏠
Augusta Rule — Rent Your Home
💰 $10K–$28K/year completely tax-free
Rent your home to your business up to 14 days/year tax-free. You never report the income. Your business deducts the rent as a legitimate business expense.
IRC §280A(g) ↗
👨‍👩‍👧
Hire Your Children
💰 Up to $15,750 per child — tax-free income
Pay children reasonable wages for real work. They pay zero federal tax on the first $15,750 (standard deduction). Your business deducts the wages. No payroll taxes if under 18 in a sole prop.
IRC §162 + §3121(b)(3) ↗
🏥
HSA Triple Tax Shelter
💰 The only triple tax-free account in the tax code
Pre-tax contribution + tax-free growth + tax-free medical withdrawals. After 65, withdraw for anything at ordinary income rates. Max $4,300 individual / $8,550 family in 2025. The most underused account in America.
IRC §223 / IRS Pub 969 ↗
📉
Tax Loss Harvesting
💰 Offset unlimited capital gains with losses
Sell losing investments to offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar. $3,000/year ordinary income offset. Unlimited carry-forward. Repurchase equivalent assets after 30 days (wash sale rule).
IRC §1211 + §1091 / IRS ↗
🎁
Charitable Remainder Trust
💰 Donate + deduct + tax-free sale + income for life
Donate appreciated assets to a CRT. Get an immediate deduction. CRT sells tax-free, invests, pays you income for life. Remainder goes to charity at death. Zero capital gains on the sale.
IRC §664 / IRS CRT Guide ↗
🏛️
Estate Tax Shield — $15M OBBBA
💰 $30M per couple passes completely tax-free
OBBBA permanently raised the estate exemption to $15M per person ($30M per couple). Combined with stepped-up basis, a couple can pass $30M+ with zero estate or capital gains tax — permanently.
IRC §2010 / IRS Estate Tax ↗
